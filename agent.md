# Dual Perspective Digital - Content Generation Guidelines

This document serves as a reference for generating consistent, on-brand content for the Dual Perspective Digital website and all marketing materials.

## Core Motto

**"Fast. Specialized. Visually Striking."**

This motto should be integrated naturally throughout the website and content. It represents our three core pillars:
- **Fast**: Rapid execution, especially for time-sensitive projects
- **Specialized**: Highly specialized expertise and custom solutions
- **Visually Striking**: Exceptional visual design and aesthetic appeal

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Professional yet approachable**: Technical expertise without jargon overload
- **Confident but not arrogant**: We know what we do well, and we communicate it clearly
- **Direct and clear**: No fluff, straight to the value proposition
- **Boutique positioning**: Small studio, big impact—emphasize personalization and close relationships

## Key Differentiators

- **Speed + Quality**: We don't sacrifice quality for speed—we deliver both
- **Specialization**: Deep expertise in specific areas, not generalists
- **Visual Excellence**: Design is not an afterthought—it's core to what we do
- **Event Expertise**: Specialized knowledge in event technology and displays (hinted, not explicit)
- **Boutique Approach**: Close, honest relationships from day one
- **Time-Sensitive Projects**: Built to handle urgent deadlines and fast turnarounds

## Event Expertise Positioning

**Important**: Event expertise should be **hinted at, not explicitly stated** as the primary focus.

- Mention event solutions as one capability among others
- Reference "events, fairs, exhibitions" naturally in context
- Don't position as "an event technology company"
- Frame as: "We work on specialized projects, including interactive displays for events"

## Language Preferences

- **Primary**: English (en)
- **Secondary**: Spanish (es)
- All content must be translated and maintain the same tone
- Spanish translations should feel natural, not literal
- Maintain consistency in terminology across languages

## Technical Terminology Standards

### Preferred Terms
- "Custom websites" (not "websites")
- "Interactive displays" (not "digital signage" - too generic)
- "Messaging platform integrations" (not "chat integrations")
- "Specialized solutions" (not "custom solutions" - emphasizes expertise)
- "Fast execution" (not "quick delivery" - more professional)
- "Visually striking" (not "beautiful" - more impactful)

### Terms to Avoid
- Generic terms like "digital solutions" without specificity
- Overly technical jargon that excludes non-technical readers
- Buzzwords like "cutting-edge" or "revolutionary"
- Vague promises without substance

## Content Structure Guidelines

### Hero Section
- Lead with value proposition
- Include the motto naturally
- Emphasize specialization, speed, and visual appeal
- Keep it concise and impactful

### Capabilities
- Focus on what makes each capability special
- Emphasize the specialized nature
- Mention speed and visual design where relevant
- Be specific, not generic

### Approach
- Emphasize speed and specialization in each step
- Mention time-sensitive project handling
- Highlight visual craft and design excellence
- Show how we're different from generalist agencies

### FAQ
- Address common questions about specialization
- Emphasize speed and fast execution
- Mention event projects naturally
- Highlight chatbots and messaging integrations
- Show expertise without being boastful

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Do's
- Use active voice
- Be specific and concrete
- Show, don't just tell
- Use "we" confidently but not arrogantly
- Emphasize partnership and collaboration
- Mention speed naturally, not as a gimmick

### Don'ts
- Avoid passive voice
- Don't use vague claims
- Don't overpromise
- Don't use corporate speak
- Don't hide behind generic terms
- Don't make speed sound like a compromise

## Example Phrasing

### Good
- "Specialized solutions, executed fast, designed to impress"
- "Highly specialized projects delivered quickly"
- "Interactive displays that combine visual impact with technical precision"
- "Built for fast execution on specialized projects"

### Avoid
- "We do everything" (too generic)
- "Cutting-edge solutions" (buzzword)
- "We're the best" (unsubstantiated claim)
- "Quick and cheap" (undermines quality)

## Maintaining Consistency

When generating new content:
1. Reference this document first
2. Ensure the motto is naturally integrated
3. Emphasize the three pillars (speed, specialization, visual appeal)
4. Hint at event expertise without making it the focus
5. Maintain boutique positioning
6. Use preferred terminology
7. Keep tone professional yet approachable
8. Translate to Spanish maintaining the same tone

## Questions to Ask When Creating Content

- Does this emphasize our specialization?
- Does it communicate speed without compromising quality?
- Does it highlight visual excellence?
- Is event expertise hinted at naturally (if relevant)?
- Does it maintain our boutique positioning?
- Is the tone consistent with our brand voice?
- Would this resonate with clients who need fast, specialized, visually striking solutions?
